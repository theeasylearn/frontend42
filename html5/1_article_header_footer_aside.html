Paneer butter masala
Ingridents
  1. Paneer (cottage cheese) : 200g (cubed)
  2. Butter : 2 tbsp
  3. Oil : 1 tbsp
  4. Onion : 1 large (finely chopped)
  5. Tomatoes : 3 medium (pureed)
  6. Ginger-garlic paste : 1 tsp
  7. Green chilies : 1-2 (slit)
  8. Cashew nuts : 10-12 (soaked in warm water for 20 minutes and ground
    9. to a paste)
  9. Fresh cream : 2 tbsp
  10. Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) : 1 tsp (crushed)
  11. Fresh coriander leaves : for garnishing
  12. Turmeric powder : ½ tsp
  13. Kashmiri red chili powder : 1 tsp
  14. Coriander powder : 1 tsp
  15. Garam masala : ½ tsp
  16. Salt : to taste
  17. Sugar : ½ tsp (optional)

Cooking Instructions

  1. Preparing the Paneer:
    1. Heat 1 tbsp of butter and 1 tbsp of oil in a pan.
    2. Lightly fry the paneer cubes until golden.
    3. Remove and set aside.
  2. Make the Masala Base:
    1. In the same pan, add the remaining butter.
    2. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
    3. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, sauté until the raw smell goes away.
    4. Add tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  3. Add the Cashew Paste:
    1. Mix in the cashew paste, stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
    2. Cook for 2-3 minutes on medium heat.
  4. Spice it Up:
    1. Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, salt, and sugar.
    2. Mix well and cook for another 5 minutes until the masala thickens.
  5. Add Cream and Paneer:
    1. Lower the heat and stir in the fresh cream.
    2. Add the fried paneer cubes and simmer for 5 minutes on low heat.
  6. Finish with Kasuri Methi and Garam Masala:
    1. Add crushed kasuri methi and garam masala.
    2. Stir well and cook for a couple of minutes.
  7. Garnishing:
    1. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.

Serve with: Butter naan, roti, or jeera rice.

Paneer Butter Masala is a popular and indulgent North Indian dish, known for its creamy, mildly spicy, and rich tomato-based gravy. It features soft paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cubes cooked in a smooth sauce made from butter, tomatoes, cashew paste, and a blend of aromatic spices. The dish is further enriched with fresh cream and kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves), which add depth of flavor and fragrance. Paneer Butter Masala pairs beautifully with Indian breads like naan or roti and can also be served with rice. Its luxurious texture and vibrant flavors make it a favorite in Indian cuisine.

Paneer Pahadi
Ingredients
  1. Paneer (cottage cheese): 200g (cubed)
  2. Thick curd (yogurt): ½ cup
  3. Fresh coriander leaves: 1 cup
  4. Mint leaves: ½ cup
  5. Green chilies: 2-3
  6. Ginger: 1-inch piece
  7. Garlic: 5-6 cloves
  8. Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves): 1 tsp
  9. Roasted besan (gram flour): 2 tbsp
  10. Lemon juice: 1 tbsp
  11. Mustard oil: 1 tbsp
  12. Turmeric powder: ¼ tsp
  13. Cumin powder: 1 tsp
  14. Chaat masala: 1 tsp
  15. Garam masala: ½ tsp
  16. Salt: to taste
  17. Oil: for grilling or frying

Cooking Instructions

  1. Prepare the Marinade:
    1. In a blender, add fresh coriander, mint leaves, green chilies, ginger, and garlic.
    2. Blend to form a smooth paste.
    3. In a bowl, mix the paste with thick curd, roasted besan, kasuri methi, lemon juice, mustard oil, turmeric, cumin powder, chaat masala, garam masala, and salt.
  2. Marinate the Paneer:
    1. Add the paneer cubes to the marinade and mix gently to coat the paneer pieces evenly.
    2. Cover and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes in the refrigerator.
  3. Grill or Fry the Paneer:
    1. Heat oil in a pan or on a grill.
    2. Cook the marinated paneer on medium heat, turning occasionally, until all sides are golden and slightly charred.
  4. Garnishing:
    1. Garnish with lemon wedges, onion rings, and fresh coriander leaves.

Serve with: Green chutney, naan, or roti.

Paneer Pahadi is a flavorful and aromatic North Indian dish, where paneer cubes are marinated in a vibrant mixture of fresh herbs like coriander and mint, along with spices, yogurt, and besan. After marination, the paneer is grilled or fried until slightly charred and golden. The dish has a refreshing and mildly spicy taste, making it an excellent choice for appetizers or a light meal. Paneer Pahadi pairs well with green chutney and is often served with naan or roti, perfect for an authentic North Indian experience.
