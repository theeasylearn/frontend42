Paneer Butter Masala

Paneer Butter Masala is a rich and creamy North Indian dish made with soft paneer cubes simmered in a luscious tomato-based gravy. Infused with aromatic spices, butter, and a touch of cream, this dish pairs perfectly with naan, roti, or rice. A restaurant-style favorite, it‚Äôs easy to make at home and perfect for any special meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp butter (unsalted)
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 green cardamoms
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 clove
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 2 large tomatoes (chopped)
  • 10-12 cashews
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 1 cup water
  • Salt to taste
  • 200g paneer (cubed)
  • 2 tbsp fresh cream
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Gravy

  1. Heat 1 tbsp oil and 1 tbsp butter in a pan.
  2. Add bay leaf, cardamoms, cinnamon, and clove. Saut√© for a few seconds.
  3. Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cashews, cook until tomatoes turn mushy.
  6. Add red chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Let this cool, then blend into a smooth paste.

Step 2: Cook the Gravy

  1. Heat the remaining 1 tbsp butter in the same pan.
  2. Add the blended gravy and cook for 3-4 minutes.
  3. Add 1 cup water, mix well, and simmer for 5 minutes.
  4. Add garam masala, Kasuri methi (crush it between your palms), and sugar. Mix well.

Step 3: Add Paneer & Finish

  1. Add paneer cubes and cook for 2 minutes.
  2. Pour fresh cream and mix gently.
  3. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

Paneer Pasanda

Paneer Pasanda is a rich and indulgent North Indian dish made with stuffed paneer slices, pan-fried to perfection, and served in a creamy, flavorful gravy. This restaurant-style delicacy is perfect for special occasions and pairs well with naan, roti, or jeera rice.

Ingredients

  • 200g paneer (cut into slices)
  • 2 tbsp grated paneer (for stuffing)
  • 2 tbsp chopped nuts (cashews & almonds)
  • 1 tbsp raisins
  • 1 tbsp cornflour
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 2 large tomatoes (chopped)
  • 10-12 cashews
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 1 cup water
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp fresh cream
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Stuffed Paneer

  1. Mix grated paneer, chopped nuts, and raisins to prepare the stuffing.
  2. Place a small portion of stuffing between two paneer slices, creating a sandwich.
  3. Coat the stuffed paneer with cornflour.
  4. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a pan and shallow-fry the stuffed paneer until golden brown. Set aside.

Step 2: Prepare the Gravy

  1. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a pan, add chopped onions, and saut√© until golden.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes and cashews, cook until soft.
  4. Blend the mixture into a smooth paste.
  5. Heat the same pan, add the blended gravy, and cook for 3-4 minutes.
  6. Add red chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Add 1 cup water and simmer for 5 minutes.
  8. Add garam masala, Kasuri methi (crush it between your palms), and sugar.

Step 3: Combine & Serve

  1. Gently place the fried paneer sandwiches in the gravy and cook for 2 minutes.
  2. Pour fresh cream and mix lightly.
  3. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
